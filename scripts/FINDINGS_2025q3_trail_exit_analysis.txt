FINDING 1: Winners Are Dramatically Larger in 2025_Q3
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• 2024_Q3 & 2025_Q1: Avg winning trade ~2.1-2.4%, with only 2-3 trades > 5%
• 2025_Q3: Avg winning trade 5.39%, with 6 trades > 5% (includes +16.6%, +15.1%, +14.5% winners)

This is not due to lucky entry timing—it's that the baseline Supertrend flips are capturing multi-day trending moves.
Trail exits add value by exiting BEFORE reversals, but if the move is trending cleanly (+15% with no -3% pullback),
the trail has nothing to improve upon.

FINDING 2: 2025_Q3 Trades Show Minimal Pullback Structure
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Avg loser size is UNCHANGED (−1.77% to −2.33% across all quarters)
• But winning trades are 2.3× larger, suggesting the trend environment is cleaner

In 2024_Q3 and 2025_Q1, you had: small wins, frequent pullbacks → trail mechanisms could catch early exits
In 2025_Q3, you have: large wins, sustained trends, few pullbacks → trails trigger after ST already exited

FINDING 3: Long-Hold Win Rate Peaks in 2025_Q3 at 82.4%
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
• Trades held >17 hours (200 bars): Q3'24 66.7% WR, Q1'25 55.6% WR, Q3'25 82.4% WR

This indicates that 2025_Q3 trades are riding extended directional moves with minimal mean-reversion.
A trail mechanism's main edge is *mean-reversion exit* (exit before bounce back). When the regime is
strongly trending, there's less bounce-back to exit on.

FINDING 4: The Math Shows Why +3.5pp Is the Ceiling
────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2025_Q3 baseline: +3.96% return | 15 wins @ avg +5.39% | 27 losses @ avg −1.77%

For a trail mechanism to improve this meaningfully, it would need to:
1. Exit a winner +1-2% early (before reversal) = −1 to −2% value loss per trade
2. Avoid the largest winners (+15%+) being stopped out prematurely

With only 6 large winners and 9 small winners, the marginal value from exiting small winners at +1-2% instead of 0%
is: 9 trades × +1% = +9% potential, but this is theoretical. In reality, grid search found only +3.5pp improvement,
meaning the best variants could only consistently add ~+0.08% per trade—true redundancy.
